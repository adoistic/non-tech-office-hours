# Moats

**Category:** Umbrella — moats / defensibility
**Source:** raw/nfx-network-effects-manual.md, raw/yc-helmer-7-powers-transcript.md

## Definition

A moat is a structural advantage a well-funded competitor cannot simply buy or engineer their way past. It persists through a full product cycle, not just the launch window. Buffett named the concept; Helmer's 7 Powers is the canonical post-2000 framework for naming specific moat types.

## Mechanism

- **Network effects:** each new user increases value for existing users; the incumbent benefits more from growth than any challenger.
- **Scale economies:** unit costs fall as volume rises; the incumbent is permanently cheaper at scale.
- **Switching costs / lock-in:** customers face high friction — financial, operational, or psychological — when leaving; the incumbent retains revenue the challenger cannot capture.
- **Brand:** accumulated reputation creates a price premium or demand preference the challenger cannot copy at any engineering budget.

Helmer's 7 Powers (post-2000 canonical framework) adds Counter-Positioning, Cornered Resource, and Process Power. See [[counter-positioning]], [[cornered-resource]], [[process-power]].

## When it applies

- A competitor with 10x capital and a 1,000x-engineer team cannot trivially replicate the position.
- The advantage persists through a full product cycle — not just during the launch window.
- At least one mechanism above is the proximate cause of retention or pricing power, not just "we are first."

## Kill-test

If a competitor with 10x your capital and a 1,000x-engineer team showed up tomorrow, what specifically stops them from replicating your position?

## AI-native erosion check

Code-as-moat has collapsed. Pre-AI, engineering effort and architectural novelty were treated as defensibility. Post-AI, a greenfield rebuild that took six months in 2023 takes a weekend. Data, network effects, switching costs, brand, and regulatory positions have held up. Engineering-effort claims have not. See [[ai-native-moat-shift]] for the full analysis.
