# Cornered Resource

**Category:** Helmer Power
**Source:** raw/yc-helmer-7-powers-transcript.md

## Definition

A cornered resource is a coveted asset (patent, regulatory position, proprietary data, embedded relationship) that competitors cannot replicate at any price or within any reasonable time horizon. The classic form is a pharma patent. The AI-era form, per Garry Tan: "the corner resource doesn't have to be a diamond mine. It could be the diamond mine in your customer's heads."

## Mechanism

- The asset is independently valuable and not arbitrageable: competitors cannot simply outspend to acquire it.
- Access creates a durable cost or capability advantage that widens with continued use — regulatory clearance compounds with embedded relationships, proprietary data compounds with model improvement.
- The forward-deployed engineer model is the current primary form: months of embedded access to a customer's operations produces proprietary prompts, evals, and fine-tuned data that no competitor can replicate without the same access.

## When it applies

- Your resource is exclusive, either legally (patent, contract) or practically (regulatory approval process, relationship depth that takes years to build).
- A competitor with 10x your funding could not acquire the same resource in two years.
- The resource improves or compounds the longer you hold it, rather than depreciating.

## Kill-test

Could a well-funded new entrant acquire the same resource by signing a check or hiring three of your people? If yes, you have an advantage, not a cornered resource.

## AI-native erosion check

Cornered resource held up well. The forward-deployed engineer model is a native AI-era expression: startups that embed engineers with customers gain access to private data, workflows, and domain knowledge that cannot be purchased or scraped. Scale AI's DoD relationships and the "brain space" in government decision-makers are Garry Tan's cited examples. Fine-tuned proprietary models are a second form: Character AI reduced inference cost 10x through fine-tuning on data competitors do not have. The threat is if foundation labs restrict model access — that would make the underlying model itself a cornered resource held against startups.
