# AI-Native Moat Shift

**Category:** Cross-cutting — AI era
**Source:** raw/nfx-network-effects-manual.md, raw/yc-helmer-7-powers-transcript.md

## Definition

The structural change in which moats based on engineering effort — "we built it faster," "our architecture is novel," "this took us 18 months" — have collapsed to near-zero defensibility, while moats based on data, distribution, network effects, operations, regulatory position, and brand have held or strengthened.

## Mechanism

- LLM-assisted development delivers roughly 1,000x engineering capacity: a greenfield rebuild that took six months in 2023 takes a weekend in 2026.
- Autonomous coding loops and agentic IDEs make software factories freely available. Code is now a commodity input, not a scarce asset.
- What could not be replicated cheaply in 2023 now can be. The exceptions are network effects (value lives in relationships, not code), accumulated proprietary data (not copyable by script), distribution relationships (human trust), regulatory positions, and brand — none of these are code-solvable.
- The gap widens further as 1M-context models mature. Founders anchored on 2022–2023 defensibility frames are systematically over-rating their moat.

## When it applies

- Any time a founder's defensibility claim rests on engineering effort, architectural novelty, or speed of initial build.
- Most acutely: B2B SaaS, dev tools, vertical AI wrappers — categories where incumbents historically used "we built it first and it's hard to build" as a primary moat claim.
- Applies regardless of how technically sophisticated the product is.

## Kill-test

Of your claimed moat, how much rests on code or engineering speed (vulnerable) vs. data, network, distribution, ops, regulatory, or brand (durable)? Name the percentage and the specific mechanism for each bucket.

## AI-native erosion check

This page IS the erosion check for every other moat type. For this page itself: the shift is structural and ongoing. The gap between pre-AI and post-AI defensibility widens as autonomous coding loops mature. There is no scenario in which code-as-moat recovers. Founders anchored on 2023 frames will keep over-rating their position until a well-funded challenger proves it in their market.
