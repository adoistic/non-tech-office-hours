# LinkedIn — Market Network Effects

**Concept:** [[market-network-effects]]
**Source:** raw/nfx-network-effects-manual.md

## What they built

LinkedIn is also a market network: it connects professionals who transact with each other (hiring, freelancing, consulting, business development) through a platform that enhances a professional network that already existed offline. Unlike a pure marketplace, LinkedIn's nodes maintain identities and communicate directly; transactions are often facilitated but not completed on-platform.

## The mechanism

- Each professional on LinkedIn is both a potential buyer and seller of labor and services, creating many-sided value rather than a clean two-sided buyer/seller split.
- LinkedIn's SaaS tools (Recruiter, Sales Navigator) layer workflow software on top of the identity graph, deepening embedding beyond the social layer.
- Network density per industry vertical makes the market network self-reinforcing: recruiters in finance need LinkedIn because financial professionals are on LinkedIn, and vice versa.

## Why the analog often fails

Founders building a "market network for X" often confuse it with building a marketplace. LinkedIn is instructive precisely because the transaction is not on-platform: LinkedIn facilitates discovery and communication but does not close the hire or the deal. If a founder's market network requires completing the transaction on-platform to generate revenue, the business model needs a different design. The other common miss is that LinkedIn's market network effect only works because the identity graph (personal network effect) came first; a market network without a pre-existing identity layer is just a directory, not a defensible market network.
