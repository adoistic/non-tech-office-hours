# Bitcoin — Protocol Network Effects

**Concept:** [[protocol-network-effects]]
**Source:** raw/nfx-network-effects-manual.md

## What they built

Bitcoin is an open-source distributed ledger protocol launched in 2009 that enables peer-to-peer transfer of a scarce digital asset without a central intermediary. Miners validate transactions and earn newly issued bitcoin; wallet holders hold and transfer value. No single company owns the protocol.

## The mechanism

- Every wallet, exchange, and custodian that integrates the Bitcoin protocol increases the liquidity and utility for all existing holders, compounding network value.
- Thousands of derivative products (ETFs, DeFi bridges, Lightning channels) are denominated in or pegged to bitcoin, making the Bitcoin protocol the base layer other networks build on.
- Switching requires convincing every counterparty to switch simultaneously; the coordination cost grows with network size, making displacement nearly impossible.

## Why the analog often fails

Founders citing Bitcoin as a protocol comparable usually miss two things. First, Bitcoin's protocol network effect is inseparable from its belief network effect: the value of the asset is partly circular (people hold it because others hold it), not purely utilitarian. A founder's protocol without a scarce asset or coordinated belief layer loses half the mechanism. Second, no single entity captured the value: Satoshi's stake notwithstanding, the protocol creator cannot monetize directly through pricing or access control. If a founder expects to "own the protocol" and extract margin from it, the analogy requires a centralized control point that Bitcoin deliberately lacks.
