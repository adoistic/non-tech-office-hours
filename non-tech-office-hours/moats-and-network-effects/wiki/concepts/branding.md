# Branding

**Category:** Helmer Power
**Source:** raw/yc-helmer-7-powers-transcript.md

## Definition

Brand is a moat when a company becomes so well known and trusted that consumers choose it over functionally equivalent alternatives purely on the strength of the name. As Harj Taggar summarizes Helmer: "brand is essentially a moat when you become so well known that even if you have an equivalent product, consumers will still choose you because of the brand effects." The Coca-Cola example is canonical. In AI, OpenAI's ChatGPT brand now exceeds Google's Gemini in daily consumer usage — even though Gemini 2.5 Pro is considered by many practitioners to be an equivalent model.

## Mechanism

- Trust accumulated over time lowers the activation energy for a consumer to choose the brand without conducting a fresh comparison.
- Brand compounds: early brand wins attract users, whose usage generates more data, whose word-of-mouth reinforces the brand identity with new cohorts.
- A strong brand shifts the default: a consumer who hasn't thought hard about the category picks the known name, not the better-reviewed alternative they'd have to research.

## When it applies

- The product has reached category-defining scale — consumers name your product when asked to name the category.
- The brand carries emotional trust or identity signaling beyond the functional product.
- Consumer switching requires overcoming inertia, not just finding a better product.

## Kill-test

If your product were relaunched tomorrow under an unknown name with identical features, would your current customers find and choose it within 90 days? If yes, you have customer satisfaction, not brand power.

## AI-native erosion check

Brand is the hardest Helmer power to build in the AI era because it takes time — time that moves slowly relative to the pace of product iteration. That said, the power is unchanged once established. OpenAI ChatGPT's brand dominance over Google Gemini, despite Google's larger installed user base, shows brand can be built by a newcomer even against a historically dominant consumer brand. For early-stage startups, brand is rarely an available moat; the transcript is explicit: "I think in the AI context, it's probably harder to apply brand as a moat directly to startups. It just takes time."
